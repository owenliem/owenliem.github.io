About

I am an incoming M.A. student in economics at Columbia University. My research spans development, labor, and health economics, with further work in law and economics, matching theory, and political economy.

Much of my empirical work uses Indonesian and Southeast Asian survey data to study how policy shapes labor markets, human capital, and well-being, from school construction and childcare access to severance pay and gender norms. Alongside this, I work on theoretical questions in judicial remedy design and in matching markets with imperfectly transferable utility.

Before Columbia I earned a Bachelor of Economics from Universitas Gadjah Mada and held research roles at Bank Indonesia and the Center for Microeconomics Research and Dashboard. You can reach me at oal2119@columbia.edu.
